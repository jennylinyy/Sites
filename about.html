At a great risk to themselves and their loved ones back in China, a group of Chinese-Americans established Epoch Times in the United States in May 2000. Some reporters in China were jailed, and some suffered severe torture, all to bring true information to the world.

Epoch Times launched in 2000 with a Chinese-language print edition in New York and a website. Local editions published by bureaus around the world soon followed. Today, Epoch Times is the largest Chinese-language newspaper outside of mainland China and Taiwan.

Epoch Times in the English language launched in September 2003 on the web, and published its first print edition in New York in August 2004. Our English edition is the leading English-language authority on China news, providing reporting on China with extraordinary insight that you can’t find anywhere else.

Today, print editions of English Epoch Times can be found in major cities across the United States, Canada, the UK, Ireland, Australia, Malaysia, Singapore and Hong Kong.

Due to our exclusive political and financial market coverage on China, Epoch Times English Edition is read by affluent executives who have business interests in China and the Asia region.
